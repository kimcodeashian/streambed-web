    • Getting Started

    Opening an account on Streambed

    • Access the sign-up page
    • Create a username
    • Enter email address
    • Create password
    • Confirm password entry
    • Agree to Streambed’s Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.
    • The user will receive a verification email to activate their account on the email address they entered during the sign-up process
    • Click the link in the email from Streambed to activate account; users will be directed to a login page
    • Once the account is activated, the user can access their account

    Connecting your Streambed account to Zoom

    • Once the Zoom app is connected to Streambed, the user is able to see their saved Zoom videos
    • The user is able to select saved Zoom videos, and assign collaborators or contributors to the content
    • Once the user assigns collaborators/contributors to the video, a record of the video’s existence and assigned collaborators is published to the FLO blockchain
    • The user can then publish this video to YouTube, allowing collaborators to see the real-time performance of the video in Streambed’s analytics dashboard
    • Once Zoom and YouTube are connected, the user will be able to assign contributors and analytics access to all videos posted from Zoom to YouTube via Streambed

    Connecting your Streambed account to Youtube

    • Once the Youtube app is connected to Streambed, the user is able to upload videos to Youtube directly via Streambed
    • The user is able to assign collaborators or contributors to the content
    • Once the user assigns collaborators/contributors to the video, a record of the video’s existence and assigned collaborators is published to the FLO blockchain
    • The user can then publish this video to YouTube, allowing collaborators to see the real-time performance of the video in Streambed’s analytics dashboard

    Connecting your Streambed account to Twitter

    • Once the Twitter app is connected to Streambed, the user is able to post tweets via Streambed
    • The user is able to draft tweets and assign collaborators or contributors to the content
    • Once the user assigns collaborators/contributors to the tweet, a record of the tweet’s existence and assigned collaborators is published to the FLO blockchain
    • The user can then post the tweet on Twitter, allowing collaborators to see the real-time performance of the tweet in Streambed’s analytics dashboard

    Connecting your Streambed account to Facebook

    • Once the Facebook app is connected to Streambed, the user is able to post on Facebook via Streambed
    • The user is able draft content and assign collaborators or contributors to the content
    • Once the user assigns collaborators/contributors to the Facebook post, a record of the post’s existence and assigned collaborators is published to the FLO blockchain
    • The user can then post this to Facebook via Streambed, allowing collaborators to see the real-time performance of the video in Streambed’s analytics dashboard

    Connecting your Streambed account to Instagram

    • Once the Instagram app is connected to Streambed, the user is able to draft Instagram posts through the Streambed app
    • The user is able to create new posts and assign collaborators or contributors to the content
    • Once the user assigns collaborators/contributors to the post, a record of the post’s existence and assigned collaborators is published to the FLO blockchain

    Upload Your Content

    How to upload to Youtube

    • Make sure your Youtube Account is connected to Streambed
    • Upload by clicking upload icon and following prompts
    • Assign contributors (or credits) to the YouTube video by by entering each contributor’s name and email address into the system - an example of this would be assigning a “contributor” role to every person featured in the video, along with the crew
    • Click “next”
    • On the summary screen you’ll see the title and confirmation of your video
    • Once you return to the dashboard, you’ll see your uploaded video in the Content Stream.
    • The video will be immediately available on your YouTube account

    Confirming Contributors

    1. Each contributor receives an email asking them to verify their participation by clicking a linked ‘verify’ button
    2. Contributors do not have to create an account to verify their participation
    3. Once a contributor becomes verified by clicking the verify button, they are presented with the option to create a free account and see the real-time analytics of the YouTube video

    Using the Content Stream

    Content and User Streams

    1. Sign into the Streambed app using your login
    2. Click the graph icon near the top right of your screen to access your analytics dashboard
    3. Click the ‘Content Stream’ category on the right side of your screen where it says ‘select content’
    4. Scroll down to see all the videos that you have produced, contributed, or co-created.
    5. Filter the content stream to see what videos you want to see the analytics for. (Streambed automatically selects all content for you, so you must filter manually in order to see analytics for one piece of content.)
    What is the content stream?

    The content stream is a place where you can see all the content being uploaded via streambed. You can filter the content based on what platform it was posted on.

    What does the filter function do?

    From here, you can actually search for keywords of videos you're looking for.

    How do I know what my role was on each piece of content?

    At the bottom of every video in the content stream, it will tell you whether or not you are a publisher, co-creator, or contributor.

    Will all my content show on the content stream?

    Yes, however it will only show for whatever app that the filter is applied to.

    Example: If the filter is applied for ‘YouTube’ it will only show videos posted on YouTube.

    All About Blockchain

    What is the FLO Blockchain?

    FLO is a public record for building decentralized applications that utilizes metadata attached to each transaction. This metadata layer allows reading and writing to the blockchain quickly and easily, encouraging unique apps and tools to be built with FLO. Each block is processed in intervals of 40 seconds. FLO is a fork of litecoin which retains very similar characteristics to how Bitcoin functions.

    Open Index Protocol

    Open Index Protocol (OIP) is an open source specification for a persistent worldwide index and file library useful for data publishing, file distribution and facilitating direct payments. OIP uses blockchain technology and distributed networking to operate with no central authority: record indexing, file storage/distribution and transaction management are carried out collectively by the decentralized network.

    OIP is the first permissionless system with a decentralized and transparent index for digital content and protected file persistence. The system uses a Salutary Protocol model, which funnels financial incentives to both application and protocol layers, ensuring sustainability and antifragility of the system through open market incentive alignment of all participants.

    The OIP specification describes:

    • How a proof-of-work blockchain and interoperable transport protocols create a shared data layer for digital content
    • How metadata, terms of use, file addressing, and payment information are stored in the blockchain
    • How to search and browse the shared data layer
    • How the blockchain security incentive is connected to the value of the content published to the shared data layer
    • How file persistence & legal compliance are ensured

    How do OIP and FLO work together?

    Open Index Protocol uses the Flo blockchain for the meta-data index and interoperable transport protocols for file storage/distribution and payments. Current implementation supports IPFS for file storage/distribution and Bitcoin, Litecoin and Florincoin tokens for payments; support for additional file and value transport protocols is on the roadmap.

    Uninstalling & Deactivating

    How to delete your account

    1. Visit Profile > Account Settings
    2. Click “Deactivate Account”
    3. Click “yes I’m sure”
    4. Write down your mnemonic phrase
    5. Then click “yes” - your account is now deleted.

    What we do with your data

    Your data is stored, encrypted, on the blockchain. We can never take it down, that’s one of the most important features of streambed. However, we cannot decrypt the information without your consent. If you deactivate your account, we have no way to identify, use, or see any of your information. For more on what we store and how we use it, see our privacy policy.

    Account recovery

    Make sure when you deactivate your account to write down your mnemonic phrase. This phrase is then entered into our security protocols to reinstate your account. Put this phrase somewhere safe - do not store it digitally. Write it down on a piece of paper and don’t show it to anyone.

    To recover your account:

    1. On app.streambedmedia.com/signup, click “restore account.”
    2. Type in specific words of your mnemonic phrase according to the prompts
    3. Re-log into your account and change your password
    4. Reconnect your social media accounts

    Mnemonics & Account Restoration

    What is a mnemonic phrase?

    A mnemonic phrase, mnemonic recovery phrase or bitcoin seed is a list of words which stores all the information needed to recover a bitcoin wallet. A wallet will typically generate a mnemonic backup phrase and instruct the user to write it down on paper. If the user's computer breaks or their hard drive becomes corrupted, they can download the same wallet again and use the paper backup to get their bitcoins back.

    In many cases a mnemonic phrase is preferred as a way to make it easier to access a wallet since instead of individual characters/numbers, it’s almost as if it were a sentence made up of words.

    How it works

    The software of a wallet uses a whole set of words taken from a vocabulary; at that every word is assigned to a specific number. A mnemonic phrase can be modified into a number, which is used as a seed that generates all pairs of keys for this wallet. The English word list for the BIP39 standard has 2048 words. If a phrase consists of 12 words, then the number of possible combinations is 2048 to the 12th power, or 2 to the 132nd power, i.e. the phrase will have 132 bits of safety.

    Theoretically, one can think out the mnemonic phrase by his own, but it is not safe, because a human cannot do a great job with the random generation. The best way to secure one’s wallet is to take a mnemonic phrase generated by the program code.

    FAQ

    Am I able to connect multiple Youtube accounts to my Streambed account?

    To keep our analytics accurate for each account, we only allow one type of social media account per Streambed user.

    Why can’t I see the analytics from some of my posts?

    On the right side of the screen, make sure you have selected the videos/posts you want to see the analytics for. If you would like to access the total overall analytics, click “select all.”

    Why aren’t my previous posts and videos on Streambed?

    The app will populate your 40 previous items from each of your social media platforms, but it will not create streambed pages for them. You must go through and generate index pages for each to have their analytics show up in your feed.

    What if I enter my contributors wrong?

    In future builds of streambed you will be able to edit contributors. For now, take your video down and try again.

    What does Streambed have to do with the blockchain?

    Blockchain proofing is at the heart of how we are able to do what we do. For more on this, check out our Chairman Michael’s article on our Blog.

    Troubleshooting

    My account isn’t connecting to Youtube

    • If you have used Streambed in the past, you may need to reset the app’s permissions. To do this, follow the instructions from Google: https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/3466521?hl=en. Once you have completed the steps listed, use our instructions to reconnect your Youtube account.
    • Make sure your youtube account is only connected to one streambed account. Social accounts can only connect to one user account at a time.

    My account isn’t uploading videos

    need links 'Account Limits'
    • Is the social channel you’re trying to connect to connected to streambed? If no, visit how to connect my accounts.
    • Make sure your video isn’t too long. Youtube only accepts videos shorter than 30 minutes, and twitter only accepts videos shorter than 3 minutes.
    • If you get to step 3 and the upload stalls, you may have reached your upload limit for the day. Visit Account Limits for more information.

    My email verification link expired!

    If you don’t click the link to activate your account within 24 hours, it will expire and so will your account. Register again using the same username and it should work. If not, send us an email and we’ll reset it for you.

    I typed my contributor’s email wrong!

    Currently we do not support editing tags on contributors. This is a feature we aim to implement in the next few months. For now, take your video down and upload it again with the correct information.

    My dashboard won’t load and just has the endless spinning teal “loading” loop.

    • Make sure your social accounts are all connected.
    • Log out, shut down your browser, and try again
    • Clear your cache and try again
    • If you can’t solve this, contact support and we’ll get back to you within 24 hours.
    About account limits:

    Youtube only allows you to upload 20 hours of video per day, so Streambed does the same. In future we will be developing scheduling capabilities, but until then just come back each day and continue on.
